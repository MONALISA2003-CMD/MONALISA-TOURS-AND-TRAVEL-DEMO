Where the bookings actually go

Ten destinations, mapped off our own departure point

Every route on this page is drawn from Entebbe, because that's where almost every one of our trips actually begins. Flight times are approximate and depend on the season's routing.

hover a route for detail · all times approximate, confirmed at booking

Safari
01.06° S, 29.66° E

Bwindi Impenetrable Forest, Uganda

Eight habituated gorilla families across four trailheads. The trek runs forty minutes to four hours depending which family draws your permit. Pack gloves, the nettles are real.

Jun–Aug, Dec–Feb
Safari
02.27° N, 31.78° E

Murchison Falls, Uganda

The Nile narrows to about seven metres before the drop, loud enough that launch guides stop talking until you're past it. Tree-climbing lions on the north bank show up often enough that guides will reroute a drive on a tip.

Dec–Feb, Jun–Sep
Beach
06.16° S, 39.30° E

Zanzibar, Tanzania

Stone Town's alleys are easy to get lost in on purpose. The east coast tide drops far enough twice a day to walk to the seaweed farms, ask which afternoons line up before booking a sunset dhow.

Jun–Oct, Dec–Feb
Cultural
33.92° S, 18.42° E

Cape Town, South Africa

The Table Mountain cable car closes for wind more often than first-timers expect, so we build a backup morning into every itinerary. Stellenbosch wine country is about forty minutes by car.

Nov–Mar
Cultural
31.63° N, 7.99° W

Marrakech, Morocco

Jemaa el-Fnaa is a different place at 4pm than at 9pm, we send first-timers in the evening and tell them to agree a price before any photo with a snake charmer. Medina riads are quieter than the name suggests once you're past the door.

Mar–May, Oct–Nov
Honeymoon
36.39° N, 25.46° E

Santorini, Greece

Oia gets the famous sunset crowd. Imerovigli has the same view with a quarter of the people. We route most honeymoon clients to the latter and send them to Oia at lunchtime instead.

Late May–Jun, Sep–Oct
Wellness
03.20° N, 73.22° E

Maldives

Seaplane transfers only fly in daylight, so a late inbound flight can mean an unplanned night near Malé. We flag this at booking, before it becomes a surprise at the airport.

Dec–Apr
Luxury
46.02° N, 7.75° E

Zermatt, Swiss Alps

Zermatt bans private cars, you arrive by train and the silence surprises most guests on the first night. The Matterhorn Glacier Paradise cable car is the easiest 3,800 metres you'll ever gain, and still cold up there in July.

Jul–Sep, Dec–Mar
Cultural
35.01° N, 135.77° E

Kyoto, Japan

Fushimi Inari has no opening hours and almost no crowd before seven in the morning. The arcade at Nishiki Market beats most nearby restaurants for lunch, and costs less.

Mar–Apr, Nov
Luxury
25.20° N, 55.27° E

Dubai, UAE

The Old Dubai souks along the creek are a fifteen minute abra ride from the skyline most people picture, and a better afternoon than the mall. Desert evening safaris book out fastest October through March.
