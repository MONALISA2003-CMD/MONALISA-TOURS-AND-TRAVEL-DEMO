Safari · Photography

Gorilla Trekking & Photography Safari

Lake Mburo, two trekking days across two Bwindi sectors, then Queen Elizabeth for the Kazinga Channel. Built so a photographer gets two distinct gorilla families rather than one rushed encounter.

$2,840per person, land only · 7 days
DAY
1

Entebbe to Lake Mburo

Arrive Entebbe International, met by your driver-guide. Drive south west to the Lake Mburo area, about four and a half hours, with a stop in Mbarara town. Evening boat ride on the lake if you land early enough.

DAY
2

Mburo game drive, on to Bwindi

Morning game drive for zebra, eland and impala, species you won't see again further south. Continue to Bwindi, roughly four hours, arriving in time for the trek briefing.

DAY
3

Gorilla trek, Buhoma sector

7am briefing at park headquarters. Trek length depends entirely on where your assigned family is that morning, anywhere from forty minutes to four hours each way. Free evening back at the lodge.

DAY
4

Gorilla trek, Ruhija sector

A different sector and a different family, which is the point of booking two days rather than one. Optional Batwa cultural walk in the afternoon for an additional fee.

DAY
5

To Queen Elizabeth, via Ishasha

Drive north with a detour through the Ishasha sector, where lions climb into fig trees during the heat of the day, no guarantee but a strong chance. Afternoon game drive on arrival.

DAY
6

Kazinga Channel launch, Kasenyi plains

Morning boat cruise along the channel for hippos, buffalo and a long list of waterbirds. Afternoon game drive across the Kasenyi plains for lion and the resident hyena clan.

DAY
7

Fly back to Entebbe

Morning at leisure, then a short scheduled flight from Mweya airstrip back to Entebbe, about ninety minutes against five or six hours by road. Connect with your international departure.

Included

Mid-range to upscale lodge accommodation, 4x4 with driver-guide throughout, all park entry fees, the activities listed above, breakfast, lunch and dinner daily.

Not included

Gorilla permits (around $700 per person, paid directly to Uganda Wildlife Authority and non-transferable), international flights, visa fees, tips, alcoholic drinks, travel insurance, the optional Batwa walk ($15pp). Single supplement +$340.

The part nobody enjoys reading

Cancellation and payment, in plain terms

A booking is confirmed once a 30% deposit clears. For trips involving gorilla permits, that deposit becomes non-refundable as soon as we purchase the permit, since the Uganda Wildlife Authority does not refund or transfer permits once issued, usually within a few days of your deposit.

For everything else, cancelling more than 60 days before travel gets a full refund minus a $60 admin fee. Inside 60 days, the refund depends on what's already been paid to lodges, airlines or transfer operators on your behalf, we will always tell you the exact figure before you decide, not after.

The remaining balance is due 30 days before departure. We accept Visa, Mastercard, bank transfer, and MTN or Airtel Mobile Money for deposits originating in Uganda or Kenya. International card payments carry a 2.5% processing fee, shown at checkout.

General guidance, always confirm directly

Entry requirements at a glance

Rules change by nationality and by destination, this is a starting point, not a substitute for checking with the relevant embassy or consulate before you travel.

Uganda & East Africa

Yellow fever vaccination certificate required on arrival. Many nationalities can use the East African Tourist Visa, covering Uganda, Kenya and Rwanda, applied for online before travel.

Schengen destinations

Passport should be valid at least three months beyond your departure date from the Schengen area, and ideally six. Visa requirements depend on your nationality.

UAE

Many nationalities receive a visa on arrival or visa-free entry for short stays; we confirm your specific case before booking flights.

Japan & Switzerland

Visa-exempt for a number of nationalities for short tourist stays; six months' passport validity is the safest assumption for both.
